What Do I Wear?

Concepts and Vocabulary

Learn New Words

Read about clothes.

The things you wear are called clothes. On a cold day, you might wear pants, a shirt, and a sweater. On a warm day, you wouldn't need a sweater. You wear shoes or sneakers on your feet every day.

Speaking and Listening

Read and Respond

Take turns reading with a partner.

SUSANA: I will wear warm pants.
LUCAS: I will wear a warm shirt.
SUSANA: I will wear warm shoes.
LUCAS: I will wear my sweater.
SUSANA: I will wear clothes that keep me warm.

Grammar and Conventions

Write Sentences

Use a and an before one person, place, or thing.
• Use a before words starting with a consonant.
• Use an before words starting with a vowel.
Use the before a specific person, place, or thing.

Complete these sentences. Use a, an, and the.

1. On a warm day, I don't wear a coat.
2. I am wearing ______ shirt she gave me.
3. I like ______ clothes she wears.
4. I am wearing ______ old pair of pants.
5. I'm looking for ______ new pair of shoes.
